Venn×

A Java (JavaFX) desktop app for creating Venn diagrams.

This app was the end result of my four-month "Software Development Project" course, in which we were divvied up into groups of four (in my case, two) to complete our capstone project. It was a simulation of a client-developer workflow, so we obtained our instructions by asking our non-technically-inclined "stakeholder" what he wanted from the application. In the end, we were told that our application was "the best in the class by far" by our professor, and "everything I wanted and then some" by the stakeholder. It was written 100% in Java. We used JavaFX for the UI and TestFX for automated testing.

Carsurance×

An ML-based mobile app for getting car insurance estimates.

This app was the final product of my very first hackathon! I was in a group with three other people whom I had never met before. We came up with the idea to create an app that uses machine learning to estimate different car insurance quotes from multiple different companies, since we all know how much of a pain it is to try to compare quotes. I was responsible for the back-end work. This was my first time working with Python, Google Cloud, and Flask; I learned a lot and I really enjoyed it! I have never had so little sleep in one weekend, but I also had a ton of fun.

Shrug×

A SwiftUI iOS app for helping you make decisions with a spinner.

This was my second quarantine project, inspired by the fact that my family can never decide what to order in. This app lets you customize a spinner, so you can input all your restaurant choices (or any other options to pick between) and let the app decide for you. It has been very useful so far! I would like to polish this app up and eventually publish it to the App Store. It's made with 100% Swift and SwiftUI. I'm very proud of how far it has come! Development is currently on pause, due to my current employment contract that restricts me from publishing software independently.

Solitaire×

A SwiftUI iPad/Mac Catalyst solitaire game.

In the first few weeks of quarantine, I wanted to challenge myself. So, I decided to work on a multi-platform SwiftUI app for macOS (using Catalyst), iOS, and iPadOS. I went for Solitaire, because it's a game I know well. This was a much more difficult project than I expected initially (which is part of why it's still in-progress), but it has been very fun to work on. It works best on macOS for now, but I am working on iPadOS support next, with iOS to follow that.
